About Us

Our story, mission, and vision for creating possibilities in Nakivale Refugee Settlement.

Our Story

Department of Possibility (DoP) is an indigenous, non-profit, humanitarian organization founded in 2022, with an aim of empowering both refugees and host communities through targeted interventions that address critical socio-economic and health needs. It is based in the Nakivale Refugee Settlement, located in Isingiro District, Uganda. Nakivale Refugee Settlement is one of the oldest refugee settlements in Uganda, hosting a diverse population from various countries, each facing significant challenges related to displacement, poverty, and access to essential services.

With a vision rooted in creating opportunities and building capacity, DoP focuses on innovative and impactful programs that enhance livelihoods, promote community health, and address the impacts of climate change. The organization integrates these interventions with emerging issues such as gender, conflict Mitigation and Human Rights. Guided by principles of equity, inclusion, and community participation, DoP collaborates closely with stakeholders, including OPM, Local Government, international organizations, and community members, to ensure its programs are responsive to the needs of the population it serves. By leveraging the strength of partnerships and community-led solutions, DoP aims to transform lives and nurture hope in Nakivale and beyond.

Our Mission

To empower communities by promoting health equity, advancing climate resilience, and fostering sustainable livelihoods through innovative programs, collaborative partnerships, and evidence-based solutions that create lasting impacts for people and the community.

Our Vision

We seek a world that is centered on dignity where every person deserves the same opportunity, freedom and choice.

Our Manifesto

"We believe in the inherent potential of every individual, regardless of circumstance."

We are committed to creating spaces where refugees can be architects of their own futures, not just recipients of aid.

Our work is rooted in community knowledge, sustainable practices, and the belief that solutions come from within.
